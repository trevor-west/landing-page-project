Iki Island
Island of the Gods

Iki Island is a remote island in southwest Japan. It has a rich history and culture with over 1,000 Shinto Shrines, delicious food, and breathtaking natural scenery.

About the Island
Iki Island Culture and History

This is the iconic giant monkey rock of Iki Island, known in Japanese as "Saruiwa" (猿岩).

Original ancient carved stone of a human face, called the "Jinmenseki" (人面石) in Japanese, which is thought to have been used for ancient spiritual rites.

This is Amenotenagao Shrine (天手長男神社) on Iki Island, one of over 1,000 shrines that encompass the island!

This statue depicts the ancient samurai warrior Yuriwaka (百合若大臣) fighting the leader of the Oni monsters known as the Evil King (悪毒王)

Iki Island: a wondrous island with a rich history, culture, and people. With over 1,000 Shinto Shrines, breathtaking scenery, and delicious food - this island truly is blessed by the gods.
